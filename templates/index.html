Greyhounds have been tied to gambling in Florida since 1931 but this year could be different.

A Senate-proposed gambling bill, SB8, unbinds the race dogs from the gaming industry.

A national tax-exempt non-profit, GREY2K, argues greyhound racing is a dying industry and is trying to outlaw the sport in Florida and across the country.

In 2013, the organization worked to implement a rule that would require greyhound race-dog trainers to report when dogs die at race tracks and on kennel grounds to the state. Since then, 383 dogs have been reported dead.

These are the most accurate data the state has but it is self-reported. Two of Florida's 12 race tracks have a relatively low numbers of reported deaths on their premises since 2013: Naples-Fort Myers Rack Track and Melbourne. The average number of deaths per track is 32. There have been just three reported deaths at the Naples track and six reported deaths at the Melbourne track.
